07 · Content Principles

What Makes Content Work.

These aren't guidelines. They're the difference between content that gets watched once and content that sells for months.

01
The Taste Reaction is the Ad
Electrolytes+ wins because it tastes extraordinary for something with nothing in it. Film your first sip unscripted. Film the first sip of a friend who's never tried it. That reaction is worth more than any talking-head explanation about electrolyte science. Lead with sensation, always.
02
Entertain First, Educate Second
If you open with "Electrolytes are important for hydration," you've already lost them. Open with something that makes someone pause the scroll. The taste, the routine, the unexpected detail. Then, once you have them, teach them something. In that order, every time.
03
Show the Ritual, Not the Product
The most powerful frame is: "I can't start my morning without this." Not "this product has B vitamins." Ritual content converts because it creates aspiration and relatability simultaneously. Your audience doesn't want to buy a supplement — they want to live the way you live.
04
Pre-Workout+ is a Category Redefinition
Every existing pre-workout is built for a gym bro. Pre-Workout+ is built for someone who trains with intention — reformer, mat, barre, tennis. Position it that way. "This is the first pre-workout that doesn't feel wrong in a Pilates studio" is a story that no competitor can tell. Own it.
05
Show the Stack When You Can
Featuring both products in one piece of content tells the complete brand story. Morning Electrolytes+, pre-class Pre-Workout+. It's a higher-converting format because it shows the full system, and it doubles the AOV when someone clicks through to buy.
06
Authenticity is the Production Value
Real moments outperform produced ads. A real taste reaction in a kitchen that looks lived-in will outperform a perfect studio setup with a scripted line. Your voice, your style, your space. That's the content brief. Everything else is optional.
